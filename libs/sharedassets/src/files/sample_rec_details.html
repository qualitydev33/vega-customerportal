Public Cloud Providers provide an abundance for resource families and sizes curated to meet the needs of modern engineering teams. The overabundance of options can often lead to resources being over provisioned, or even when properly utilized there may be an option available that provides better performance for less, this is the case with most generational or processor refreshes.

Vega analyses your usage, CPU, and Memory utilization over a 30 day period to provide multiple options to allow end users to select the resource type best suited for their needs, while driving cost savings.

 

If you agree to this recommendation, Vega will perform these actions:

Generation Update - These are traditional generation updates, recommend migrating current resources from an older generation to the latest.
Right Sizing - Traditional right sizing looks at downsizing an instance to more effectively utilize available Memory and CPU.
Processor Refresh - Looks at migrating off of Intel to the cheaper processor types of Graviton or AMD based on the current resource configurations.
Cross Family Right Sizing - Utilizing CPU and Memory metrics of the current resource running, Vega Recommends migrating to resources in different families and types to optimally size a resource for performance and cost.
Optimal Recommendation - Based on the optimal performance, cost benefit, and customer risk tolerances, Vega provides the optimal resource type from the above recommendations.

* Please note: when looking at CPU & Memory metrics Vega utilizes a full suite of metrics over the last 30 days, if a material portion of metrics are missing or no metric data is available at all, Vega assumes 100% utilization when making recommendations.
